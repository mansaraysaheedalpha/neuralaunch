Recommendation No. 0427 Cycle I · Path I (No Idea) Ready

Spend the next three weeks proving demand for the maternal-care advisory line. Not building the app.

Confidence High

You came in with an app idea and a stalled clinic. Eleven turns in, the picture that emerged is different — and clearer. You have a clinical network, a real WhatsApp following, and a shortage of hours. The app eats two hundred hours before anyone says yes.

A three-week paid advisory pilot, run from your existing channel, will tell you within thirty days whether to build at all — and at what price. The build question becomes answerable. You will not be guessing anymore.

I.Reasoning

Why this — for you — and not anyone with the same idea.

You have the rarest input the app concept needs: warm, local clinical authority in a market where that is scarce. Most people pitching maternal-care apps in Sierra Leone have neither the credentialing nor the existing audience. You have both.

What you do not have is two hundred unfunded engineering hours. The roadmap you came in expecting — design, build, launch — assumes time you have repeatedly told us you do not have. Eight hours a week, for six months, fits an advisory pilot. It does not fit an app build.

The pilot also creates the input the app would need anyway. Real customers, at real prices, saying real things. That data is what makes a future build de-risked. Skipping it makes the build a bet.

II.First three steps

Where you'll be by Friday week.

  1. Frame the pilot as a 30-day paid advisory line. One page: who it's for, what they get, what it costs. Not a website — a WhatsApp-broadcast-able pitch.
    Est. 2 hours · uses Service Packager
  2. Recruit ten existing followers at NLe 200 / month. From your existing list. Not strangers. Use the Conversation Coach to rehearse the recruit message before you send it.
    Est. 4 hours over 1 week · uses Outreach Composer · Conversation Coach
  3. Run the first paid consultations via WhatsApp Business. Booked, paid, delivered. Capture what they asked, what they paid for, what they actually wanted.
    Est. 6 hours / week × 3 · check-ins after each
III.Time to first result

Fourteen days — first paid consultation booked.

Sized against the eight weekly hours you stated. If a follower says yes within the first batch of ten outreach messages — which the prior is high enough to expect — the booking is live inside two weeks. If they don't, you know inside two weeks. Either result is useful.

IV.Assumptions

Three assumptions this recommendation rests on. Flag any one to see how the recommendation changes if it turns out to be false.

Your existing WhatsApp following includes at least thirty active mothers or expecting mothers who would consider paying for advisory.
If false: The recruitment phase becomes the work — we'd reframe Phase 1 around audience-building (referral-driven) before pricing. Time to first paid consult moves from 14 days to ~28.
You can run consultations from WhatsApp Business without violating clinical regulations in Sierra Leone.
If false: We'd pivot the channel to a regulated tele-health partner (Aspen, mPharma) and use the pilot to validate price, not channel. Build the partner conversation in front of the customer one.
The audience values availability and warmth over clinical depth at this price point.
If false: The price drops to NLe 100 and the offer becomes triage-with-escalation, not advisory. Different positioning entirely — closer to a free-tier with paid escalation.
V.Risks
● High Audience cold to paid offers. The current following may consume free content but balk at paying for advisory. The pilot is structured to surface this inside the first week. Week 1
● Medium Regulatory grey zone. Distance consultation isn't explicitly forbidden but isn't explicitly allowed either. Run the first batch quietly while you check. Ongoing
● Low Time slippage. Eight hours / week is a stated number, not a measured one. If actual hours are five, the pilot still works — it just runs four weeks instead of three. Weeks 2–4
VI.What would make this wrong

The condition for changing course.

If fewer than three of the first ten outreached followers say yes at price — even with the Conversation-Coach-prepared message — the advisory frame is wrong, not the channel. Pivot to free-tier triage with escalation to a paid clinic visit, not a paid advisory line.

VII.Considered & rejected

Build the app first, then find customers.

Rejected because the build absorbs 200+ hours you do not have, and because the app's core assumption — that the audience will pay for guided maternal advice — is the exact thing the pilot tests for free.

Open a second physical clinic location.

Rejected because the constraint that stalled the first clinic — your time — is not solved by adding capacity. More chairs do not help a sole practitioner.
