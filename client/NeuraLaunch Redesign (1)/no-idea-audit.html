Pipeline audit · Path I (No Idea) 6 stages · 0 through 5 Read · 2026-05-26

Six stages.
Six surfaces.
One system.

You shipped the hard part. The pipeline works end-to-end — Mindset, Outcome, Requirements, Pain Inventory, Opportunity Evaluations, Handoff. What's wrong is not the architecture; it's that six different surfaces look like six different products. Each was built with care and the shadcn defaults available at the time. Together they don't yet read as one institute.

The pipeline does institute work. The surfaces still read as SaaS product.

The Mindset page is a Markdown brief in a `max-w-2xl` column. Stage banners are dismissable info-toasts in `bg-card/40`. The skill canvas is a four-lane drag-and-drop tier list with `border-success/40` colour-coding. The opportunity canvas is a stack of cards with chevron-toggle expansion. All of it is competent. None of it earns the word premium.

Below — the verdict for each stage, what to keep, what to rebuild, and the order I'd do it in.

What's there

A page-of-text with a button.

Three section blocks — What you're about to do · What this requires · What you'll get — set in the default sans, on a `max-w-2xl` column. The CTA fires a server action that creates the session and routes to Stage 1.

What's wrong

It's a brief, not a welcome.

  • The CTA is a quiet line at the bottom: "Begin, when ready →". No "primary button" appearance.

I.
Outcome Definition.
What outcome would fit your life. 4 dimensions · streaming chat · OutcomeDocument
Verdict
● Redesign first
What's there

A chatbot in a wrapper.

Streaming question → user reply → next question, in a generic message thread. A dismissable banner at the top describes the four dimensions: time horizon · financial goal · risk tolerance · lifestyle preference. The OutcomeDocument appears when readiness ≥ 75% for pre-commit review.

What's wrong

The first chat looks like every other AI chat.

This is the moment the founder starts to feel whether the institute is going to listen to them. Right now it feels like talking to a chatbot. The four dimensions are invisible unless they re-read the banner. There's no sense of where they are in the four or what's left.

The treatment
  • Adopt the discovery-a pattern: question front-and-centre in big sans, answer field below, belief panel on the right.
  • The belief panel shows the four dimensions as roman numerals (I → IV) lighting up as confidence accumulates per axis.
  • OutcomeDocument review stays — but rendered as a one-page memo with serif headings, not a card stack.
  • Banner becomes a session stamp, not a dismissable toast.

II.
Requirements.
What you're built to execute. 14 skills × 4 tiers · DnD canvas · per-teammate tabs · Expected Profile derivation
Verdict
● Redesign first
What's there

A four-lane drag-and-drop tier list.

14 skill chips drag into 4 colour-coded lanes (good · acceptable · bad · unknown). Teammates added via tabs. The Expected Profile (what the outcome demands) is derived by Sonnet from Stage 1 and shown alongside. Founder pushback opens a drawer.

What's wrong

The colour-coding is doing the wrong work.

The lanes are green/blue/red/grey. The page reads like a project-management board. This is a self-assessment instrument, not a kanban. The reds especially are uncomfortable to look at — the founder is staring at their weaknesses in destructive-action colour.

The treatment
  • Hairline grid, no fills. Lanes are columns with monospace labels: Strong · Adequate · Weak · Unknown.
  • Chips are typography, not pills. Skill name in sans, tier indicated by the column it's in. One accent dot per chip in strong; nothing in weak except italic style.
  • The Expected Profile renders as a faint overlay row above the founder's row — a "what's demanded" ghost over the "what's there" reality.
  • Teammate tabs become roman tabs: I. You · II. Daniel · III. Add teammate.

III.
Pain Inventory.
Where the real pain is. Pain Scout (8-step Sonnet) + founder additions · 3-axis scoring · shortlist cap 5
Verdict
● Then
What's there

Two columns, three sliders per card.

The Pain Scout fans out across community_pulse · Tavily · Exa and surfaces candidate pain points. The founder adds their own via a form. Each card has three sliders — intensity · frequency · niche specificity — used to score viability. Up to 5 viable points unlock composition.

What's wrong

The cards all look the same.

An agent-surfaced pain that 800 people complained about in a Reddit thread looks identical to a pain the founder typed in from memory. The source is in a small chip; the signal strength is buried. The most important visual hierarchy in this stage is "where did this come from and how loud is it" — currently invisible.

The treatment
  • One ledger, not two columns. Each pain on its own row. Italic-roman source label (I. Scout · II. You) marks origin.
  • Score is rendered as dots, not sliders. Five dots per axis = 5/5. Click to adjust; immediate, calm.
  • Signal strength is a typographic weight — heaviest signal gets the largest type, weakest is set in muted italic.
  • Scout re-runs become an action of intent: a numbered call with a single editorial line — "Run No. III · 8-step Sonnet · Tavily + Exa".

IV.
Opportunity Evaluations.
The docket. Layer A research (6-step) · Layer B test scripts · per-opp verdict · pushback
Verdict
● Heavy lift
What's there

Stack of expanding cards, one per opportunity.

Five opportunities. Each has a chevron-toggle card with Layer A research findings (4-dimension), a Layer B test script + community responses (text or screenshot), a verdict picker (5 verdicts), and a push-back drawer. The most architecturally complex surface in the product.

What's wrong

Five opportunities collapse to five identical chevrons.

The whole point of Stage 4 is that the five opportunities are not equal. Their Layer A signals differ. Their Layer B validation strength differs (verified / likely / contradictory / unverified). Their founder verdicts will differ. The current UI treats them as equal-weight cards in a stack — the most important signal in the whole pipeline (which one is winning) is hidden behind clicks.

The treatment
  • The docket. Five opportunities in one ledger, always visible. Each row: roman index, name, Layer A status, Layer B strength as a typographic glyph (●●●○ = strong, ●○○○ = contradictory).
  • The expanded view goes full-page, not inline. Open one opportunity, see Layer A + B + verdict picker at hero scale. ESC returns to the docket.
  • Verdicts visible in the docket as a stamp column — "advance," "drop," "park," "needs more."
  • "Re-derivation needed" banner becomes a hairline strip at the top of the row, not a tinted card.

Cross-cutting.
Build once, ship everywhere. Banners · chat shell · readiness meters · cascade strips

● Small
Three components shared across all stages

Build them once. The system improves everywhere.

Each stage page currently rebuilds the same primitives in slightly different ways. Lift them into the shared component layer and every redesign downstream becomes a config change, not a rewrite.

  • <StageBanner> — the dismissable "what this stage is" intro. One implementation, accepts stageNumber, title, body, sessionId. Same dismiss + persistence behaviour, new typography.
  • <StageInterview> — the streaming chat shell. Owns the question, the textarea, the submit, the keyboard shortcuts, the voice button, the safety/error states. Plugs into Stage 1, 2, 3 (and the standard Discovery path) via a single `messages` + `onSend` prop.
  • <BeliefRail> — the right-side belief panel. Accepts an array of fields with status (captured / live / unknown) and a completion percentage. Wire to OutcomeDocument fields in Stage 1, SkillInventory in Stage 2, PainPoints in Stage 3 (with a different label set).

Estimate · 2-3 days of focused work. Pays for itself on the first stage you redesign after, because the shared shell carries the new aesthetic for free.

Six stages. One system.

The hard work — the agents, the durable jobs, the cascade machinery, the pain scout, the layer-A research — is shipped. The work that remains is typographic and structural, not architectural. The cross-cutting components alone make the next four redesigns easy.

I'd start with the Stage 1 Outcome Definition redesign as a built example, since it's the highest-leverage surface in the pipeline and reuses the discovery-a pattern almost directly. Say the word and I'll build it.
