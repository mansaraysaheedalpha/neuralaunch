§ 01The cycle

One arc. From first question
to first outcome.

01

Be heard.

A 4-phase interview builds a fifteen-field belief state. Up to fifteen questions. Audience-aware. Capped, so it ends.

Discovery Orientation → goals → constraints → conviction. Hard cap at 15.
02

One recommendation.

Synthesised across the full belief state. With reasoning, risks, assumptions you can flag, and the alternatives that were rejected — and why, for you specifically.

Phase 1B emission Opus 4.6 reasons, Sonnet formats. No menus. No hedge.
03

Push back.

Up to ten rounds of real argument. Defend, refine, replace. If you're right and the evidence agrees, the recommendation changes.

Pushback engine Two-phase. Hard cap triggers a constrained alternative.
04

A real roadmap.

Phased. Sequenced. Sized to the hours you actually have. Each task carries reasoning, success criteria, and the tools we'll use to get through it.

Roadmap engine Up to five phases. Up to five tasks per phase.
05

Learn. Continue.

At the end of every cycle: what happened, what we got wrong, what the evidence says, the forks ahead, the parking lot. The next cycle starts smarter than the last.

Continuation brief Five sections. Auto-generated. Ready to read.
§ 02A recommendation

This is what a real one
looks like.

Not a menu. One direction, for your specific situation, with reasoning, risks, and what would make it wrong — all on the table.

Recommendation · Cycle 01 Confidence · High

Spend the next three weeks proving demand for the maternal-care advisory line. Not building the app.

You have a clinical network, a real WhatsApp following, and a shortage of hours. The app will eat 200 hours before anyone says yes. A three-week paid advisory pilot, run from your existing channel, will tell you within thirty days whether to build at all — and at what price.

First three steps
① Frame the pilot as a 30-day paid line.
② Recruit ten existing followers at NLe 200/mo.
③ Run consultations via WhatsApp Business.
Time to first result
14 days. First paid consultation booked.

Sized against the 8 weekly hours you stated.
What would make this wrong
If < 3 of 10 followers say yes at price, the advisory frame is wrong — pivot to free-tier triage with escalation, not a paid line.
