§ 01The cycle

One arc. From first question
to first outcome. Five moments, each connected to the next.

I.

Be heard.

A 4-phase interview building a fifteen-field belief state. Audience-aware. Capped, so it ends. The quality of the recommendation is only as good as the quality of the listening.

DiscoveryOrientation → goals → constraints → conviction. Hard cap at 15 questions.
II.

One recommendation.

Synthesised across the full belief state. With reasoning, risks, and the alternatives we rejected — and why, for you specifically. Not a shortlist.

Phase 1B emissionOpus 4.6 reasons. Sonnet formats. No menus. No hedge.
III.

Argue.

Up to ten rounds of real argument. Defend, refine, or replace. If you are right and the evidence agrees, the recommendation changes.

Pushback engineTwo-phase. Hard cap triggers a constrained alternative.
IV.

A real roadmap.

Phased. Sequenced. Sized to the hours you actually have. Each task carries reasoning, success criteria, and the tool we will use to get through it.

Roadmap engineUp to five phases. Up to five tasks per phase.
V.

Learn. Continue.

At the end of every cycle, a five-section brief: what happened, what we got wrong, what the evidence says, the forks ahead, and the parking lot. The next cycle starts smarter than the last.

Continuation briefFive sections. Auto-generated. Ready to read.
