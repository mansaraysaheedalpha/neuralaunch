I.What happened

The pilot worked. Just not the way I expected.

Twenty-one days in, the picture is clear: you booked four paid consultations against a target of three. The wrong-condition we set up front did not trigger. You held pace at 9.1 hours per week — better than the eight you stated. The first revenue is real.

What surprised both of us is who said yes. The recruiter message worked best on followers who were not yet pregnant — women planning, not women three months in. The Conversation Coach prep was load-bearing on objection three, which I had marked as low-priority but turned out to be the deciding moment in three of the four conversations.

Eight tasks complete. One blocked by Meta's verification process, resolved on Day 19 without changing the plan. No recalibration offers triggered. No tasks fell more than 24 hours behind estimate. This is what executing a recommendation looks like when the recommendation was correct.

II.What I got wrong

Three assumptions did not survive.

I committed to one recommendation. That recommendation rested on assumptions. Here are the ones the evidence overturned — and what they mean for the next cycle.

Assumption overturned

"The audience values availability and warmth over clinical depth at this price point."

Actually: they wanted concrete answers, not warmth. The two conversations that converted at full price did so because you gave a specific clinical opinion. The two that hesitated did so when you stayed neutral. This shifts the offer — and the next cycle's positioning.

Assumption overturned

"Existing WhatsApp following includes at least thirty active mothers or expecting mothers who would consider paying for advisory."

Actually: the active list was closer to eighteen — but the qualifying pool was wider than that, because planning mothers (a group I did not separate) responded better than expecting ones. The audience is bigger than I sized for, in a direction I did not anticipate.

Assumption partially upheld

"You can run consultations from WhatsApp Business without violating clinical regulations in Sierra Leone."

Actually: you can — but only with disclaimers I had not anticipated. Disclaimer text is now part of every consultation. Time per session went from 45 minutes to 60 minutes as a result. Sizing the next cycle accordingly.
